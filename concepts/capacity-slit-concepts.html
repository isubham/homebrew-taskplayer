All four variants share the same rule: the track is a fixed width (160px). Under the capacity threshold, sessions sit at true scale and the bar just ends early — same as before. Once total time crosses the threshold, every session rescales down proportionally to fit the same fixed width; whatever falls past the threshold turns red; and a grey slit marks exactly where the threshold line landed. Only the slit's rendering changes between the four options below.

1 · Hairline tick

Simplest option: the bar stays one continuous piece, and a thin 2px grey line is overlaid on top at the threshold position — same mechanic as a progress marker. Cheapest to build (one absolutely-positioned div), lowest visual weight.

2 · Physical cut

The slit is a real break in the track, not an overlay — the bar visually splits into two rounded pieces (before / after the threshold) with a short grey bridge between them. Reads more like "this is where the budget ran out" than a tick mark, at the cost of eating a few px from the fixed width for the gap itself.
