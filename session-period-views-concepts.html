Today's page is one "day" strip at a time, newest first. Adding Week and Month means: (1) a period switcher at the top of the page, and (2) new aggregate visuals to replace the 24h bar, since "midnight to midnight" stops being the right shape once you're looking at 7 or 30 days. The task rollup list underneath stays the same component in all three — it just re-groups by the wider window, and expanding a task shows one row per day instead of one row per raw session (a week can easily have 15+ sessions of the same task; day-by-day is the readable granularity, not session-by-session).

Week · option A — stacked mini day-bars

Same 24h-scale bar you already have, just seven of them stacked (one per day, Mon–Sun), each still showing real session shape and color. Familiar and literal — you can see "that Wednesday was basically empty" at a glance — but it's the busiest-looking of the four options.

Week · option B — one column per day, stacked by task

A small bar chart: seven columns, bar height is that day's total, each column's own colors stacked to show the task mix. Reads faster than seven full-width bars, and scales better if Month reuses the same chart shape (below) — the "how full was each day" comparison is the point, not exact minute-by-minute placement within the day.

Month · option A — calendar heatmap

GitHub-contributions style: one cell per day, shading = hours tracked that day (darker line color = more time), laid out as a real calendar so weekday/weekend patterns jump out. Best "shape of the month" view, but it's a summary only — click a cell to jump into that day's Day view rather than expanding inline, since 30 individual day-rows would be a lot to also list below.
