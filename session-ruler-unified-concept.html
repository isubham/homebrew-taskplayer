One idea instead of three: Day, Week, and Month are the same ruler, re-zoomed — not three different chart types glued together. The same grammar repeats at every level: a continuous track spanning the period, colored session blocks positioned at their true elapsed-time location, a two-tier tick ruler underneath (bold ticks at the coarse unit, faint ticks at the fine unit), and a white "now" needle. Only the unit changes — and it nests: this level's faint minor tick is exactly next level's bold major tick. Day's hour ticks (major) are made of quarter-hour ticks (minor). Week's day ticks (major) are made of quarter-day ticks (minor) — the same four marks that are Day's own major labels. Month's week ticks (major) are made of day ticks (minor) — the same marks that are Week's own major ticks. Zoom out and you're always looking at the same instrument, one click further from the dial.

Week — same track, wider window, one level out

Nothing new is invented: it's the identical track and ruler, just spanning 7 days instead of 1. Real sessions still sit at their true position (a 9am Tuesday block lands 1/7 + 9/24 of the way across) — at this width a session is still wide enough to read as a shape, so the track keeps doing its job unaided. Major tick = day (bold, labeled Mon–Sun). Minor tick = quarter-day — the exact same four marks Day view uses as its own major hour-labels (12a/6a/12p/6p), just now living one level down as texture.

Month — the ruler takes over the track's job

Same instrument, but honestly: a 45-minute session is 0.15% of a 31-day track — under a pixel, so the colored track from Day and Week would just go dark and lie by omission. Rather than bolt on a fourth, different chart, the ruler's own minor ticks (still "1 per day," still nested inside Week's major ticks, unchanged position) pick up the one channel they were never using at closer zoom: height. Each day-tick grows with that day's total and tints toward its dominant list — the tick becomes the bar. Major ticks (weeks) stay pure structure, same as always. The now-needle still crosses it, same as always.

The recursive rule, stated plainly: zoom out one level, and the old major tick becomes the new minor tick's neighborhood. Day's hour → Week's quarter-day-within-a-day. Week's day → Month's day-within-a-week. It's the same reason a clock face doesn't need a separate widget for "hours" and "minutes" — one hand is just the other, slowed down by 12. Practically: the day/week track math you already wrote (position = elapsed / period-length) doesn't change per view, only period-length and the tick unit do — so this isn't three components to build and maintain, it's one, parameterized.
