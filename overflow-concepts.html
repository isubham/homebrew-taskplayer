A · Solid red cap (simplest)

Under budget, segments show as before. The moment you cross the estimate, individual sessions stop mattering — the whole track just goes solid red and pulses, same treatment as the battery concept from before. Exact overage is a tooltip away, not shown spatially.

Trade-off: once you're over, you lose the "which session pushed me over" detail — but that also means the bar never has to explain a confusing partial state.

B · Compress to fit

The track is always exactly full — segments resize to share whatever the new total is, so you can still see each session's relative share even once you're over. The whole thing tints red as soon as you cross 100%.

Trade-off: the bar's scale quietly changes once you're over (a pixel stops meaning a fixed amount of time), which is a small honesty cost in exchange for keeping segment detail visible.

C · Honest scale, with a marker

The scale never changes — 1 pixel always equals the same amount of time. A thin white tick marks exactly where the estimate sits, sessions before it stay green, and anything after turns red. The track itself keeps growing as long as you keep working.

Trade-off: most information-preserving option, but the bar is no longer a fixed width in the row — a task that's wildly over its estimate makes its own row wider than the others, which needs a sensible max-width/truncation rule so one runaway task can't stretch the whole table.
