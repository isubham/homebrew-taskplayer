Clicking the scrolling track title in the footer's music widget reveals what the Audius API actually gives us for that track: cover art, the artist's name, and a permalink to open it on Audius in the browser. Three placements for that reveal in-app, plus one approximation of what the same info could look like from the tray/menu-bar.

1 · Popover card above the title

Click the title, a small card pops up right above the music widget — same anchor point the old volume slider used to use before it became always-visible. Closest to a native "now playing" popover (like macOS's own Control Center music card). Click anywhere else, or the title again, to dismiss.

2 · "Now Playing" modal

Click the title, a centered modal opens — the exact same component style already used for the task-detail and settings dialogs, so it feels like part of the app's existing vocabulary rather than a new UI pattern. Biggest artwork of the three, room for a genre tag, and a full-width "View on Audius" button matching the app's existing pill-button style.

3 · Slide-down banner above the player

Click the title, a slim banner slides down and sits just above the whole footer player bar (not just the music corner) — widest layout of the three, reads almost like a toast notification. Has its own dismiss (×) since, unlike the other two, it doesn't have an obvious "click outside" area given how much of the screen it spans.

4 · Menu-bar (tray) equivalent

Native macOS menu items can't render a full card — no big artwork, no custom layout — but Tauri menu items do support a small icon next to text. Best approximation: the existing "♪ Task name" line gets a tiny cover-art icon, plus a new line right below it showing the artist name, then a clickable "↗ View on Audius" row that opens the permalink in the default browser. Clicking anywhere in the app itself still requires opening the main window first (see mockups 1–3) — the tray is a quick-glance/quick-link only, not the full details view.
